The Story of Robery Cecil Kelly


About

The positive contribution made to the Third World by my friend C.R.D. (Bob) Kelly as an international     volunteer can be summed up in one word..immense. One glance at this man's C.V. compels one to ask  how he found the time and energy to accomplish the scope and scale of his undertakings which took him to over 150 countries in 70 of which business and/or volunteer work was conducted.       I first met Bob when, a junior Trade Commissioner, he joined my staff at the Canadian Embassy, Vienna. From the get-go this young man turned in a superior performance promoting Canadian goods and services in our Eastern European territory with a special emphasis on prospects in Romania and Bulgaria. Not surprisingly his next posting was Assistant to Jean-Luc Pepin, then Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce. Several years later Bob left the Department to become co-founder of an international marketing firm with M.Pepin.       It was while travelling the world on behalf of INTERIMCO that Bob was tempted into the world that really  interested him -lesser developed countries and their educational, material and developmental needs. He later linked up with Canadian Executive Services Overseas (CESO), CARE Canada, CARE International, WUSC, World Vision and Food For The Hungry (FHI).        In Afghanistan he directed a survey to identify 1,200 families in two municipalities needing emergency food parcels from FHI and, with the World Food Plan, developed a "Food for Work" program. A personal initiative to raise funds in Canada to build several schools in Mazar-i Sharif in memory of his late wife had to be put off because of mounting hostilities in the area.       Projects in Bolivia ( design/start-up of import-export divisions in the Ministry of Economics and Trade) and Peru preceded living in Costa Rica where Bob's school- building plan was finally executed with personal and donated funds. A medical and educational complex was established in his wife's name in the remote Chirripi Valley of the Cabecar indians. A Triage Hospital was added later. The complex services 5,800 indigenous people and has already graduated arts and science students. Bob also founded an organization to fight sexual abuse of young women and the use of youth as "mules" to smuggle drugs.    Not content to rest on these laurels Bob volunteered to go to Goma, Rwanda as Director of CARE Canada and CARE International to direct operations at the local post- genocidal camp housing 850,000 refugees. Ever in danger despite having a personal bodyguard the position demanded extremes of patience in dealing with , corrupt and inept officials, perverted justice, in-house financial shenanigans, graft and death threats.The memoir of this assignment makes compelling, even terrifying, reading. Eventually a hasty, covert departure was advised.     Returning to Canada Bob quickly became involved in raising funds for the Canadian Cancer Society, set up the Philip E. Uren Memorial Scholarship Fund, worked with Rotary International and continued mentoring young people as he had in Russia, China, Taiwan, Hong Kong and Costa Rica. He also played a key role in seeing the release on parole of a falsely convicted Cree woman ( see "Stolen Life" by Rudy Wiebe).     Space does not permit my listing the many adventures Bob had over his life. Let me mention only one- A 9,000 km kayak trip from Atlantic to Pacific that prompted the writing of "SOLO", a fascinating account of his feat and his reflections and memories en route. As ever, even in difficult conditions, he lived only to be back with his beloved family who were always in his thoughts.     A final reflection. Bob's selfless lifestyle and commitment to helping others, especially the persecuted and the needy, has shown that the only courage that matters is the kind that gets you from one initiative to the next.     May his good works and sacrifices be remembered and appropriately honoured.
